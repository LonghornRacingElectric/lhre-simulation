## Pressure Effects

Pressure deltas compare each tire/rim's 14 psi fit against its 8 psi fit.

| Metric | Median 14 psi vs 8 psi | Largest drop | Largest rise |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Lateral friction abs(PDY1) | -8.8% | Goodyear D2704 20x7-13 7in (-22.0%) | Hoosier 43070 16x6-10 7in (+0.0%) |
| Longitudinal friction abs(PDX1) | -5.1% | Hoosier 43164 20.5x7-13 8in (-23.1%) | Hoosier 43070 16x6-10 7in (+3.9%) |
| Cornering stiffness / Fz | +0.4% | Goodyear D2704 20x7-13 8in (-24.0%) | Goodyear D0571 18x6.5-10 6in (+11.5%) |
| Lateral relaxation sigma | -28.0% | Hoosier 43075 16x7.5-10 7in (-35.8%) | MRF ZTD1 18x6-10 6in (-16.4%) |

Read: raising pressure generally reduces peak friction and shortens fitted lateral relaxation. Cornering stiffness is mixed, which means pressure is changing both carcass response and effective friction/load behavior rather than acting as a single clean stiffness knob.

## Rim Effects

Rim deltas compare the wider rim against the narrower rim for the same tire/model/pressure.

| Metric | Median wider-rim delta | Largest drop | Largest rise |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Lateral friction abs(PDY1) | -1.9% | Goodyear D2704 20x7-13 8 psi (-18.7%) | Hoosier 43100 18x6-10 14 psi (+2.1%) |
| Longitudinal friction abs(PDX1) | -0.3% | Hoosier 43070 16x6-10 8 psi (-18.0%) | Hoosier 43070 16x6-10 10 psi (+5.5%) |
| Cornering stiffness / Fz | +4.4% | Goodyear D2704 20x7-13 14 psi (-7.4%) | Goodyear D2704 20x7-13 8 psi (+25.4%) |
| Lateral relaxation sigma | -19.7% | Hoosier 43070 16x6-10 8 psi (-35.2%) | Hoosier 43164 20.5x7-13 8 psi (-2.1%) |

Read: wider rims usually sharpen normalized cornering stiffness and shorten lateral relaxation, but they do not automatically increase peak friction. That is especially visible on the Goodyear D2704, where the wider rim often gives up lateral peak while keeping a quick response.
