# Round 9 Fitted Combined Tire Files

Generated by `tire_fits/fit_round9_tires.py` from:

- `RunGuide_Round9.pdf`
- `RunData_Cornering_Matlab_SI_Round9.zip`
- `RunData_DriveBrake_Matlab_SI_Round9.zip`

Generated `.tir` count: `55`.

Fit policy:

- Pure lateral coefficients are fit from the tire's own Round 9 cornering runs.
- Pure longitudinal and combined-slip coefficients are fit from direct Round 9
  drive/brake/combined runs when available.
- The 16 inch Hoosier tires do not have Round 9 drive/brake/combined runs, so
  their longitudinal sections use the nearest same-pressure Hoosier
  43100 18.0x6.0-10 R20 donor, scaled by the 16 inch tire's lateral capability
  to preserve the donor longitudinal/lateral friction relationship.
- Overturning moment is a simple least-squares fit. Aligning and rolling moment
  terms not covered by this script are inherited from
  `vehicles/current/tires/16x7p5_10_12psi.tir`.
- Files use `USE_MODE = 4` to represent steady combined force/moment behavior.
  Relaxation coefficients are not newly identified from Round 9 data.
- `FIT_ASSUMPTIONS_AND_FORMULATION.md` documents the assumptions and PAC2002
  force equations used by the fitter.

Provenance:

- Direct drive/brake/combined files: `39`
- Scaled 18 inch Hoosier longitudinal/combined files: `16`
- `manifest.csv` records source runs, donor mapping, fit errors, and hashes.
- `fit_quality.csv` records the same metrics in analysis-friendly form.
